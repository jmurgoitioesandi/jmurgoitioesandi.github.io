Estimating the biomechanical properties of the cornea and ciliary body

In this study we used the velocity of shear waves propagating through the cornea and ciliary body, measured using ultrasound elasography, to characterize the hyperelastic behavior of the tissues.

  • Reference: Zhang, J., Murgoitio-Esandi, J., Qian, X., Li, R., Gong, C., Nankali, A., Hao, L., Xu, B.Y., Oberai, A.A. & Zhou, Q. (2022). High Frequency Ultrasound Elastography to Assess the Nonlinear Elastic Properties of the Cornea and Ciliary Body. IEEE Transactions on Ultrasonics, Ferroelectrics, and Frequency Control. (accepted for publication)

    • In this study they used a shaker to generate shear waves in the cornea and ciliary bodey of an ex-vivo human eye. Then they used and ultrasound transducer to measure the propagation of the shear wave. The e1 direction in Figure 1 represents the direction of the shear waves propagation. They repeated the experiment for various intraocular pressures to measure the shear wave velocity at different levels of stress in the tissues, which becomes relevant when characterizing hyperelastic behavior. Figure 2 shows the Blatz model equation, which was used to fit the experimentally measured data.

    Figure 1: Ultrasound image of the cornea indicating the directions relevant to the analysis.

    Figure 2: Blatz hyperelastic model used to characterize the cornea and ciliary body.

    In summary, although the mechanical behavior of the cornea and the ciliary body is complex, through this analysis we have deomnstrated that: the shear wave velocity measured through ultrasound ulastography can be used to infer the nonlinear hyperelastic parameters of the cornea and ciliary body and that the differences in shear wave velocity increases measured in both tissues can be explained due to the lower strain experienced by the ciliary body.
