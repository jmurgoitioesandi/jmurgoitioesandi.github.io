Summary

I have conducted research for the last seven years in various engineering disciplines. My first research project was as part of my bachelor's thesis in 2015 and I have been involved in research ever since. Currently I am part of Prof. Oberai's group at USC, Computation and Data Driven Discoveries. As part of this group, we are employing deep learning techniques to improve the current state-of-the-art of numerical methods in engineering applications.

Computational physics and machine learning

When I joined USC to start my Ph.D. studies, I started collaborating with a multidisciplinary group, which included ophthalmologists and biomedical engineers and Prof. Oberai, to improve the current understanding of Glaucoma. I first performed a computational study of aqueous humour flow to analyse the progression of primary angle-closure glaucoma (PACG). After this, I started working with conditional GANs to solve the inverse problem consisting of obtaining the biomechanical preoperties of the optic nerve head from displacements measured using an ultrasound platform. Further to this, I am also working on applying conditional GANs to solve other type of problems. The main projects that I worked on are:

  • Conditional GANs applied to ultrasound elastography of the optic nerve head.
  • Characterizing the biomechanical behaviour of the cornea using a hyperelastic model.
  • Mechanistic study of aqueous humour outflow to analyse the progression of PACG.

    • Computational and experimental fluid dynamics

    My first project in the area of experimental and computational fluid dynamics was as part of my bachelor's thesis, where I computationally studied the flow of Münster's river (Germany) for ecological purposes. After this, I conducted a computational study on the propagation of tsunami bore and an experimental study in wave group impacts on a cylinder. The main projects that I worked on are:

  • Experimental study of wave group impact forces on a cylinder.
  • Numerical study of the propagation of a tsunami bore through buildings.
  • Computational study of the flow in a river stretch for ecological purposes.
